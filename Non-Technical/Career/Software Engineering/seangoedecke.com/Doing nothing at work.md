# [Doing nothing at work](https://www.seangoedecke.com/doing-nothing-at-work/)
* If you're always 100% utilized on a steady stream of low-priority work, you'll miss out on high-impact work
  1. Too busy to notice opportunities or to hear about opportunities from coworkers, team/company updates, etc
  2. Manager won't volunteer you because it will look like you don't have capacity
* Most frantic "maybe this will help" changes during incidents make things worse, not better
* Burnout = hard work that is unrewarded
  * Going on a personal crusade that your job doesn't care about is a great way to do a lot of unrewarded work

## [HackerNews Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48442880)
* Preventing issues gets you no recognition, but building a giant pile of kindling and then putting out the inevitable fire gets you recognition twice
* Preventing an incident from happening is "what you're supposed to do"
* Kent Benk said that his team would never commit to more than half of what they think they can get done
