# [Software engineering may no longer be a lifetime career](https://www.seangoedecke.com/software-engineering-may-no-longer-be-a-lifetime-career/)
* Author uses construction work as a parallel: construction workers need to lift and carry heavy objects in order to be effective
  * Lifting heavy objects puts long-term wear on backs and joints, making workers less effective over time
  * Construction workers don’t say that being a good construction worker means not lifting heavy objects - they say “too bad, that’s just the job”
* Engineers can keep writing code by hand, but it might be harder to earn a salary doing so, for the same reason that there aren’t many jobs out there for carpenters who refuse to use power tools
* Author argues that once one stop writing code by hand, one’s ability to understand the codebase atrophies
