# [3 constraints before I build anything](https://jordanlord.co.uk/blog/3-constraints/)
* Write a one pager for all ideas
  * Potential audiences: memo for investors, contributors, team members
  * If something is not in the one pager, then it's not worth fighting over, or the one pager needs to be amended to include the item
  * If you can't fill one page, don't fill with fluff - it means that the product is not ready to be built
  * If one page is not enough, it's too complex - don't build it
* Separate any core technical ideas from the product
  * Examples: `git` was developed to improve Linux kernel development, Google built Kubernetes
  * This core tech could be a library that is extracted from the product codebase
* "Define your own constraint that is front and centre to your product"
  * The user sees and interacts with this "constraint" all the time
  * It is what gives your product identity or _feel_
  * Examples: Minecraft is built entirely from blocks, Ikea is flat-pack, self-assembly furniture
  * By not choosing this constraint, you will build a bloated product that will try to do everything
* [HackerNews Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47903541)
  * Product constraint = "product primitive"
    * Blocks in Notion, Frames/Layers in Figma, Tweets in Twitter
    * Good product design is having a small number of primitives
    * A bad product does not know what its primitives are, or has a large number of primitives
    * Product power comes from the depth from these primitives (you can do a lot with Notion blocks, Excel cells, etc)
      * Generally, highly composible
