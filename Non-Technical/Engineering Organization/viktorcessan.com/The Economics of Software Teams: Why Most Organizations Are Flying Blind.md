# [The Economics of Software Teams: Why Most Organizations Are Flying Blind](https://www.viktorcessan.com/the-economics-of-software-teams/)

## The Internal Platform Team: What Break-Even Actually Requires
* Assuming cost of 130k euro per developer per year or 65 euro per hour
* A team of eight engineers that serves 100 other engineers costs  87k euro per month
* To break even, needs to save the 100 engineers a combined 1,340 hours per month or ~3 hours per week, per engineer
* Author makes the argument that most engineers on this platform team don’t know what the break-even number is, or track it
* Author also makes the case that given a 50% initiative success rate, which is optimistic, there need to be even more wins than this break-even number to cover initiatives that fail
* Return has to cover not just the team’s current cost, but the long tail of what they leave behind - owning, maintaining, and eventually replacing these systems
* Financial viability is now between 3-5x annual cost - so need to generate 260k to 435k euro in monthly value
* Activity and sentiment metrics can trend upward while financial performance deteriorates
* A team can ship more features while building the wrong things
* Engagement scores can rise while churn accelerates among revenue-generating users
* Organizations chose these metrics because they are easier to instrument, communicate, and make look good vs. financial metrics
* Teams that measure success by features shipped, will always have something to show
* A team that measures success by generated financial returns will sometimes have to report that it does not know what the returns are, or that the returns are disappointing
* From 2011-22, zero-rate policy became normalized, and software companies could grow headcount aggressively, miss on the majority of their roadmap, and still look healthy, on paper
* There is no cohort of senior product leaders who developed their judgement in conditions where their teams were expected to demonstrate financial return, because those conditions did not exist during the years when the cohort was learning the craft
